Method of least squares

  • measured data: \((x_k, y_k)\)
  • model: \(f(x_k; a, b, \dots)\)
  • objective function:
\begin{equation*} F(a,b,\dots) = \sum_k\left[y_k - f(x_k; a, b, \dots)\right]^2 \end{equation*}
  • example: linear function
\begin{align*} F(a,b) &= \sum_k[y_k-(ax_k+b)]^2 \\ f(x_k;a,b) &= ax_k + b \end{align*}

Variations of the objective function

  • absolute vs.\ relative errors
\begin{align*} F(a,b,\dots) &= \sum_k \left|y_k - f(x_k; a, b, \dots)\right| \\ F(a,b,\dots) &= \sum_k \left[\frac{y_k - f(x_k; a, b, \dots)}{y_k}\right]^2 \end{align*}
  • weighting function
\begin{equation*} F(a,b,\dots) = \sum_k w_k \left[y_k - f(x_k; a, b, \dots)\right]^2 \end{equation*}

where \(\sum_k w_k = 1\)

  • uncertainty in data:
\begin{equation*} w_k \propto \frac{1}{\sigma_k^2} \end{equation*}

linear regression

\begin{align*} f(x;a,b) &= ax + b \\ F(a,b) &= \sum_k[y_k-(ax_k+b)]^2 \end{align*}
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial F}{\partial a} &= -2 \sum_k [y_k-(ax_k+b)] x_k = 0 \\ \frac{\partial F}{\partial b} &= -2 \sum_k [y_k-(ax_k+b)] = 0 \end{align*}
\begin{align*} a S_{xx} + b S_x &= S_{xy} \\ a S_x + b &= S_y \end{align*}
\begin{align*} S_{x} &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N x_k, \quad S_{y} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N y_k \\ S_{xx} &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N x_k^2, \quad S_{xy} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N x_ky_k \end{align*}

linear regression

\begin{align*} a &= \frac{S_{xy}-S_xS_y}{S_{xx}-S_x^2} \\ b &= \frac{S_{xx}S_y-S_xS_{xy}}{S_{xx}-S_x^2} \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} S_{x} &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N x_k, \quad S_{y} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N y_k \\ S_{xx} &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N x_k^2, \quad S_{xy} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^N x_ky_k \end{align*}
