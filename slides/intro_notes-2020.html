CP213 runs over two semesters. The lectures for this module will be myself and Abdul Sharif. Chris Boyle , Umair Jamal, and Tom Stavert will be your demonstrators.

The module will be delivered through a series of live lectures, question & answer sessions, and tutorial workshops. These will be delivered via Zoom in the first weeks of the semester, and possibly transition to face-to-face sessions later in the semester, depending on the circumstances. The links for each of these activities can be found on the Myplace site for the module.

The Monday sessions, which start at 10.00 am, will be mainly to deliver lectures of new material, while the Thursday sessions, which start at 1.00 pm, will be mainly be devoted to answering any questions that you may have regarding the course. Both these sessions will last roughly an hour.

At the beginning of each week, you will be given a tutorial sheet. Tutorial sessions will be held on Fridays from 3.00 to 5.00 to give you an opportunity to work in groups on the tutorial problems, as well as get help from the demonstrators and the lecturers.

You should start to attempt the tutorial questions before these sessions. You will be expected to upload your individual solutions to these problems on Myplace on the Monday. Fully worked model solutions will be provided in the following week.

In the final week of the semester, all scheduled sessions will be used to review the module contents as well as address any questions you may have on this or any other second year modules on the course.

A couple of recommended books which are on the reading list are Stroud, Engineering Mathematics, which is an excellent text book that covers the mathematical material we will use in this module, and Hetland, Beginning Python: From novice to professional, which is a good reference for learning the basics of Python.

Some on-line channels that contain videos that you may find helpful, are LearnChemE, which provides a bunch of lectures and worked examples on various topics in chemical engineering, developed by the University of Colorado, and 3Blue1Brown, Professor Leonard, and Eddie Woo, which are very nice YouTube channels with lectures on different aspects of mathematics.

These are just a few suggestions. If you come across any additional resources that you find helpful, please share them by posting in the class forum on Myplace.
